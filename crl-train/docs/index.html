PyTorch Causal ML Multimodal Vehicle Detection

CRL-Train

Causal Representation Learning for multimodal vehicle detection from audio and seismic sensors — a disentangled latent space that separates vehicle signatures from environmental noise.

Ground-based vehicle detection using passive sensors faces a fundamental challenge: the same sensor network must operate across a wide range of environmental conditions — rain, wind, animal activity, machinery — each of which produces interference that can mask or mimic vehicle signals. A model that memorizes sensor correlations under specific noise conditions will fail when those conditions change.

CRL-Train addresses this by learning a structured causal latent space where vehicle-relevant information (presence, type, proximity) is explicitly disentangled from noise. The system is trained to remain invariant to synthetic interventions — injected noise perturbations — so that vehicle dimensions of the latent space cannot encode noise, and noise dimensions cannot encode vehicle identity. This invariance generalizes to unseen real-world interference at inference time.

Design principle: Rather than training a classifier to be robust by augmenting with noise, CRL-Train encodes why a signal changed — whether it was a vehicle event or an environmental perturbation — into separate latent dimensions. The Structural Causal Model (SCM) over the latent space makes this causal structure explicit and auditable.
2

System Overview

The pipeline processes paired audio (16 kHz microphone) and seismic (200 Hz geophone) recordings of the same scene. Each modality is processed by an independent encoder stack — a learned filterbank, a causal temporal model, and a variational encoder — producing a structured latent vector z. A single Structural Causal Model (SCM) shared across modalities imposes a causal graph over z, enforcing that the same semantic variables (vehicle presence, type, proximity, noise) operate consistently in both sensor domains. Downstream detection and classification heads branch from the semantic blocks of z.

Data Pipeline

Raw data consists of Apache Parquet files, one per sensor node per recording session. Filenames encode metadata: {dataset}_{sensor}_{vehicle}_{rs_node}.parquet. The SensorDataset class loads these files into a flat index of fixed-length windows that the DataLoader draws from.

3-Pass Indexing (SensorDataset)

Loading happens in three passes to separate I/O from computation:

Window Extraction

At __getitem__, the dataset extracts a 1-second window from the cached waveform, resamples it to the target rate (16 kHz audio, 200 Hz seismic), and applies RMS normalization. Resampling objects are cached so torchaudio.Resample is only constructed once per unique rate pair.

RMS normalization: Each window is divided by its per-channel root-mean-square energy. This preserves the shape of vehicle transients while preventing global amplitude statistics from leaking through — a vehicle at 50 m and 200 m should produce the same latent representation modulo the proximity dimension.

Synthetic Interventions

During training, 60% of windows receive a synthetic noise injection to train the encoder to separate noise from vehicle signal. Each intervention is scaled to ~20% of the window's RMS energy so it does not completely mask the signal.

MultiHorizonPairDataset

For temporal causal training, a second dataset wraps SensorDataset to yield pairs (x_t, x_{t+n}) where n is sampled uniformly from {1 … 10} horizons. Each time step receives an independent intervention sample. The encoder must learn which latent variables changed between t and t+n without any label indicating what intervention was applied at t+n — forcing the unknown-intervention classifier to solve this purely from the latent difference z_{t+n} − z_t.

Model Architecture

Audio and seismic signals run through identical but independent encoder stacks — no parameters are shared between modalities until the latent space. The SCM, decoder, and downstream heads are shared. This design lets each sensor's front-end specialize to its frequency range while ensuring the causal graph generalizes across both domains.

LearnableFilterbank

Replaces a fixed STFT/mel spectrogram with a bank of learnable sinc-windowed FIR bandpass filters. Center frequencies are initialized in log space across the sensor's frequency range and updated by gradient descent. The squared filter output magnitude is log-compressed to give a stable envelope representation.

Structural Causal Model (SCM)

The SCM learns a directed acyclic graph over the 10-dimensional latent space. The adjacency matrix A is parameterized as a raw lower-triangular matrix; acyclicity is guaranteed by construction — no NOTEARS penalty is needed, though gradient clipping is still applied for stability. Each variable z_i is predicted by a small MLP operating on its parents z_{<i}.

Intervention masking: For a known intervention at intervention ID k, the SCM output for the affected noise dimension is replaced by the encoder's value. This tells the SCM: "don't try to explain this dim causally — it was externally perturbed." The KL for those dims is also zeroed.

Reconstructs the filterbank envelope from z. Reconstruction happens in filterbank space (not raw waveform space) to avoid phase ambiguity — the filterbank envelope is the natural output of the front-end and provides a sufficient ELBO signal without requiring a vocoder.

Latent Space Design

Rather than learning an unstructured latent space, CRL-Train pre-assigns semantic meaning to disjoint blocks of the latent vector z ∈ ℝ¹⁰. Each block corresponds to a distinct ground-truth generative factor. This makes the latent space auditable — you can probe each block independently to verify it encodes what it's supposed to — and enables targeted intervention: noise perturbations should only excite z_noise, not z_type.

z_presence 1 dim
z_type 4 dims
z_prox 1 dim
z_noise 4 dims
z_presence — binary vehicle/background
z_type — vehicle class (4 types)
z_prox — distance scalar
z_noise — unstructured environmental noise

β-Annealing Schedule

The KL weight β controls how strongly the posterior is pushed toward the standard normal prior. Training begins with β = 0 — the encoder is a standard autoencoder and encodes all information it can. β then ramps linearly to β = 1 over 40 epochs, applying progressive pressure for the posterior to use only the capacity it needs, forcing unused dimensions toward the prior and encouraging semantic specialization.

β annealing over training
β: 0.0 → 1.0 (linear ramp, epochs 0–40)
β = 1.0
Epoch 0 → 40 (annealing) 40+ (fixed)
KL zeroing on intervened dims: When a known intervention is applied to a window, the KL loss for the noise dimensions of that window is set to zero. This prevents the KL pressure from driving noise-perturbed posteriors toward the prior — a perturbed signal should have a non-standard posterior in the noise block.

Loss Functions

The total training objective combines reconstruction fidelity, latent regularization, causal consistency, disentanglement, task supervision, and structural sparsity. Each term is weighted by a coefficient λ; several of these coefficients are also annealed over training alongside β.

L_total = L_recon + β · L_KL + λ_causal · L_SCM + λ_disent · L_TC + λ_collapse · L_collapse + λ_task · (L_presence + L_type) + λ_contrast · L_contrast + λ_l1 · L_graph
TermDefault λFormulaRole
L_recon 1.0 MSE(x̂, filterbank_target) Ensures z carries enough information to reconstruct the input
L_KL β (annealed) −½ · Σ(1 + log_var − μ² − exp(log_var)) Pushes posterior toward prior; KL zeroed on intervened dims
L_SCM 5.0 MSE(z, z_scm) Forces the SCM to explain the encoder's latent structure causally
L_TC 0.5 Mean off-diagonal correlation²(z) Penalizes statistical dependence between latent dimensions
L_collapse 1.0 Penalty when log_var < threshold Prevents posterior collapse (all dims shrinking to prior)
L_presence 2.0 BCEWithLogits(ŷ_det, y_det) Supervised vehicle detection signal from z_presence
L_type 2.0 CrossEntropy(ŷ_type, y_type) Supervised vehicle classification signal from z_type
L_contrast 2.0 Variance of z across all intervention views Noise dims should vary; vehicle dims should not
L_graph 0.01 L1(adjacency matrix A) Encourages a sparse causal graph (fewer edges = more interpretable)

Fixed-β Checkpointing

Problem: The validation loss computed during β-annealing is non-stationary — the same model weights will yield a lower loss at epoch 5 (β≈0.1) than at epoch 45 (β=1.0), making epoch-to-epoch comparisons meaningless for model selection.

Solution: A second validation pass at the end of each epoch computes the loss with β fixed at beta_end = 1.0 regardless of the current training β. This fixed-β metric is the sole criterion for early stopping and best-checkpoint selection. The annealing loss is still logged for diagnosing training dynamics.
7

Training Schedule

Training proceeds in two phases: CRL pre-training (where the full pipeline including SCM is trained), followed by downstream fine-tuning (where the backbone is frozen and only the detection heads are updated). Within pre-training, a curriculum controls how quickly unknown-intervention pair training is introduced.

Curriculum: Known vs. Unknown Interventions

Unknown-intervention training (temporal pairs) is harder than known-intervention training — the model must infer which latent changed without a label. Introducing it too early, before the encoder has learned a meaningful latent space, produces uninformative gradient signal. The curriculum delays unknown pairs until epoch 10 and ramps their weight over the following 10 epochs.

Epoch rangeKnown weightUnknown weightBehavior
0 – 10 1.0 0.0 Known interventions only. Encoder warm-start; SCM begins learning causal structure.
10 – 20 0.5 0 → 0.5 (ramps) Temporal pairs gradually introduced. Unknown-intervention classifier begins training.
20+ 0.5 0.5 Equal weighting. Full causal training regime active.

Phase 2: Downstream Fine-tuning

After CRL pre-training converges, the full backbone (filterbank, SSM, encoder, SCM, decoder) is frozen. Only PresenceHead and TypeHead are updated. This validates that the learned latent space is linearly separable for the downstream tasks without any further representation learning.

Evaluation & Diagnostics

Evaluation spans three levels: disentanglement quality (does the latent space encode the right factors in the right places?), causal structure (is the SCM graph meaningful?), and task performance (does the downstream detector work?). All metrics are computed on the held-out validation set with the frozen backbone.

Mutual Information Gap (MIG)

MIG measures how exclusively each ground-truth factor is captured by a single latent dimension. For each factor v_k (e.g., vehicle type), the two latent dims with highest mutual information are identified:

MIG_k = (MI(z_top1; v_k) − MI(z_top2; v_k)) / H(v_k)

The mean MIG over all factors lies in [0, 1]. A score near 1 means each factor is captured by exactly one dimension. A score near 0 means factors are spread across many dims or multiple dims encode the same factor. MI is estimated by discretizing each z dimension into decile bins.

Linear Probe Accuracy

A logistic regression classifier is fit on the frozen latent vectors (no fine-tuning) and evaluated on the validation set. Probes are run per semantic block:

Probe targetInput blockMetricInterpretation
Vehicle type z_type (4 dims) Weighted F1 High = type encoded in z_type as intended
Presence z_presence (1 dim) Accuracy High = presence captured; low = collapse or entanglement
Full z z (10 dims) Weighted F1 Upper bound; gap from block probe = information leakage

Collapse Metrics

Posterior collapse — where some latent dimensions shrink to the prior and encode nothing — is tracked explicitly. Three complementary metrics are reported:

MetricFormulaTarget
active_dim_frac fraction of dims with std(z) > 0.05 ≥ 0.80
spread_ratio min_std / max_std across dims ≈ 1.0
active_units_frac fraction of dims with Var(𝔼[z|x]) > 0.01 ≥ 0.80

Noise Type Separation Score

A logistic classifier is trained to predict the intervention type (IDs 0–7) from each semantic block of z. This verifies the disentanglement objective directly:

Desired outcome: The classifier achieves high accuracy on z_noise (noise type is encoded in the noise block) and low accuracy on z_type and z_presence (vehicle dims are unaffected by noise injection). A high score on z_type means noise is leaking into vehicle representations — a disentanglement failure.
